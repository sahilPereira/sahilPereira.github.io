Research

My research is focused on using reinforcement learning to enable learning via imitation in the domain of autonomous vehicles. I have also worked on projects related to image processing, object segmentation/tracking and game theoretic driving models. I am interested in the research and development of machine learning models to solve practical problems.

Projects
Driving Model Based on the Stackelberg Game
Sahil Pereira

The hierarchical decision making structure of the Stackelberg model is used to model multi-agent driving as it reflects the natural hierarchies present in highway traffic beteween groups of vehicles.
Dynamic Obstacle Perception and Prediction
Ryan De Iaco, Sahil Pereira, Sushrut Bhalla

We address the problem of planning optimal paths through dynamic traffic by generating small portions of a vehicle's future trajectory using training data obtained from a video stream.

Real-time Object Segmentation and Tracking
Sushrut Bhalla, Sahil Pereira , Jaspreet Singh Sambee

Real-time segmentation and tracking of objects is addressed by modifying sub-components of the Mask R-CNN architecture. In addition, we present techniques for object re-identification after occlusions to improve tracking accuracy.

Restoration of Murals
Sushrut Bhalla, Sahil Pereira

We compare well known techniques such as patch matching and image inpainting using dictionary learning to a novel restoration technique using context encoders. The techniques are compared using No-Reference Image Quality Assessment techniques.
